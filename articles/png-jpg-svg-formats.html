PNG vs JPG vs SVG for signatures

Each format trades transparency, file size, and scalability differently. Here is how to pick without getting lost in acronyms.

PNG (raster)

PNG is ideal when you need crisp edges and optional transparency. Use transparent PNG when placing your signature over non-white backgrounds. Use white-background PNG when transparency is unnecessary but you still want lossless quality compared to JPG.

JPG (raster)

JPG uses lossy compression. It is common for photos, but for signatures it can introduce artifacts around thin strokes unless you export at high quality. JPG does not support transparency; your background will always be rectangular (typically white). Choose JPG when file size matters more than perfect stroke edges—such as email attachments with strict size limits.

SVG (vector)

SVG describes curves mathematically, so your mark can scale up for banners or down for favicon-sized uses without pixelation—when your tool exports real vector paths. SignCrafty offers SVG for drawn signatures because the strokes map cleanly to paths. Typed signatures may remain better as raster PNG/JPG depending on how your downstream tools embed fonts.

Quick selection guide

  • Email + white paper: PNG (white) or high-quality JPG.
  • Slides or branded PDFs: transparent PNG.
  • Website or multi-size branding: SVG when available from your stroke export.
  • Unknown recipient software: PNG is the safest universal choice.

Transparent PNG signatures explained

A transparent PNG keeps only your ink pixels—so your signature can sit on letterhead, photos, or slide decks without a white rectangle.

What “transparent” means

Standard photos store a full rectangular frame. A transparent PNG can store opacity: areas outside your strokes can be fully clear. That matters when your PDF page, email template, or presentation uses a color other than pure white behind the text.

When transparent PNG is the best choice

  • Colored backgrounds: marketing PDFs, posters, or branded stationery.
  • Photographic layouts: anything where a white box would be visible.
  • Layered design tools: placing the signature above artwork without re-cutting it manually.

When a white background is fine

If the final document is mostly white and you will not overlay the signature on photos, a white-background PNG or JPG can be simpler and slightly smaller in file size. Some enterprise PDF viewers also behave more predictably with non-transparent images on white forms.

Quality tips

Export at a resolution large enough for printing if needed—tiny signatures scaled up can look fuzzy. After export, open the image on a dark gray background in Preview or an image editor: you should see only ink, not a faint halo. If you see halos, regenerate from the tool with solid stroke colors and avoid excessive compression.

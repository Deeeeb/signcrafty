Digital signatures, explained

Whether you need a signature image for documents, email, or design projects, SignCrafty helps you create a clean, professional look in seconds—right in your browser.

Why use a digital signature?

A digital signature image saves time compared to scanning pen-on-paper, keeps your look consistent across PDFs and forms, and lets you reuse the same signature wherever you need it. It’s ideal when you want a visible, personal mark—not the same as a legally binding e-sign platform, but perfect for photos, contracts that accept image signatures, presentations, and creative work.

How to Create a Digital Signature Online

SignCrafty helps you build a professional signature image for PDFs, email, contracts, or design work. Use the tool above in three steps:

  1. Draw or type. On Draw, use your mouse, trackpad, or touchscreen for a natural stroke. On Type, enter your name and pick an elegant script font that matches your brand.
  2. Fine-tune the look. Choose ink color and, when drawing, line weight so the result fits your documents.
  3. Download. Export as a transparent PNG, JPG, or—when you draw—SVG for a scalable vector file.

Frequently asked questions

Is SignCrafty free to use?

Yes. SignCrafty is free to use with no signup required and no hidden fees.

Is SignCrafty safe? Is my signature stored on your servers?

Your signature is processed in your browser. SignCrafty does not upload your signature to our servers for storage. Downloads are generated locally on your device.

What image formats can I download?

You can download PNG (transparent or white background), JPG (white background), and SVG when you draw your signature (vector format).

Do I need to create an account?

No. You can create and download a signature without registering.

Is a signature image the same as a legally binding e-signature?

SignCrafty creates a signature image for documents, email, and design use. It is not the same as a regulated electronic signature service used for legal contracts; check your document requirements.
